## Definition

The Scrum Master is a servant-leader and facilitator responsible for helping the team, Product Owner, and broader organization adopt and continuously improve Scrum practices. Their primary focus is on **process, collaboration, and removing impediments** so the team can deliver value effectively.

Core accountabilities:
- **Process facilitation:** Ensures Scrum events (Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective) are productive, timeboxed, and purposeful.
- **Impediment removal:** Identifies and removes blockers that slow the team — whether technical, organizational, or interpersonal.
- **Team coaching:** Helps the team become self-managing, cross-functional, and continuously improving.
- **Scrum guardian:** Protects the team from outside interference and coaches the organization on Scrum principles.
- **Servant leadership:** Leads by serving — not by authority or status.

The Scrum Master is **not** a project manager, people manager, or technical lead. They do not assign tasks, dictate solutions, or manage people.
---

## When to Apply

- **Team is new to Scrum or struggling with process:** When ceremonies feel chaotic or unproductive.
- **Team is blocked or slowed by external factors:** Organizational impediments, dependencies, or lack of clarity.
- **Retrospectives are shallow or ineffective:** When the team is not continuously improving.
- **Cross-team coordination issues:** When multiple teams need better alignment on processes.
- **Scaling Scrum:** When helping multiple teams adopt consistent practices without losing agility.

---

## Decision Heuristics

- If the team is waiting on someone else to solve their problems, the Scrum Master should coach them toward self-resolution first.
- A good Scrum Master spends more time asking questions and facilitating than giving answers.
- When someone tries to bypass the Product Owner or assign work directly to the team, the Scrum Master should intervene to protect focus.
- Measure success by team velocity trends, quality, and how self-sufficient the team becomes over time — not by how many impediments the Scrum Master personally removes.
- If the same impediment keeps recurring, the Scrum Master has failed to address the root cause.

---

## Counter-Examples / Anti-Patterns

- **Scrum Master as project manager:** Assigning tasks, tracking progress, or creating status reports instead of coaching and facilitating.
- **Scrum Master as secretary:** Only scheduling meetings and taking notes without driving improvement.
- **Process police:** Rigidly enforcing Scrum rules without understanding the underlying principles or team context.
- **Combined PO + Scrum Master:** One person trying to own both product value and process — creates conflict of interest and usually weakens both roles.
- **Absent Scrum Master:** The role exists in name only, with no active facilitation or impediment removal.

## Prompt Snippet

```
[ORGANIZATIONAL CONTEXT: Scrum Master]
Definition: Servant-leader and facilitator who helps the team, Product Owner, and organization adopt and continuously improve Scrum. Focuses on process health, impediment removal, collaboration, and coaching.
Key distinctions:
• Scrum Master serves the PROCESS and team effectiveness
• Product Owner serves the PRODUCT and value delivery
• Scrum Master is NOT a project manager, task assigner, or secretary
• Success is measured by how self-managing and effective the team becomes over time
When relevant: Inefficient Scrum events, recurring blockers, shallow retrospectives, team onboarding to Scrum, or process-related friction.
Action guidance: Route process, collaboration, and impediment issues to the Scrum Master. The Scrum Master should coach the team toward self-resolution, remove organizational blockers, and protect the team from distractions. A great Scrum Master becomes progressively less necessary as the team matures.
```
