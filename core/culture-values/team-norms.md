## Definition

Team Norms are the explicit or implicit agreements that govern how members of a team behave, communicate, make decisions, and treat one another. They define what is acceptable and expected within the group.

Healthy teams make norms explicit rather than leaving them to be discovered through painful trial and error. Explicit norms reduce ambiguity, surface misalignments early, and create a shared reference point for accountability and feedback.

Norms can cover areas such as:
- How decisions are made and who has input
- How conflict and disagreement are handled
- Communication expectations (response times, meeting norms, candor)
- How work is reviewed and feedback is given
- Boundaries around work-life integration and respect for personal time

---

## When to Apply

- **Team formation or reset:** When a new team forms or an existing team needs to realign after growth, turnover, or conflict.
- **Recurring friction:** When the same interpersonal or process issues keep surfacing without resolution.
- **Onboarding acceleration:** When new members need to understand the team's operating style quickly.
- **Hybrid or distributed teams:** When physical separation makes implicit norms harder to observe and absorb.
- **Cross-functional collaboration:** When multiple teams must work together and need shared expectations.

---

## Decision Heuristics

- Implicit norms are still norms — they are simply the ones no one has named. Unnamed norms often favor the most powerful or longest-tenured members.
- The best team norms are co-created by the team rather than imposed from above. Ownership increases adherence.
- Norms without enforcement mechanisms or regular revisiting become decorative. They should be living agreements.
- Psychological safety is a prerequisite for honest norm-setting. People will not surface real issues if they fear punishment or exclusion.
- Fewer, well-enforced norms are better than a long list of aspirational rules that no one follows.

---

## Counter-Examples / Anti-Patterns

- **Norms as wallpaper:** Creating a beautiful team charter or set of values that no one references or holds each other accountable to.
- **Tyranny of the loudest:** Allowing the most vocal or senior members to dictate norms while quieter voices feel unable to push back.
- **One-size-fits-all norms:** Imposing the same behavioral expectations across very different teams or contexts without adaptation.
- **Norms that punish honesty:** Creating rules that make it risky to raise problems, give direct feedback, or admit mistakes.

## Prompt Snippet

```
[ORGANIZATIONAL CONTEXT: Team Norms]
Definition: Explicit or implicit agreements that define how team members behave, communicate, make decisions, handle conflict, and treat one another. Healthy teams make these agreements visible and revisit them.
Key distinctions:
• Explicit norms reduce ambiguity and enable accountability
• Implicit norms still exist and often favor those with more power or tenure
• Norms are most effective when co-created and regularly reviewed
• Good norms support both psychological safety and high performance
When relevant: New team formation, recurring interpersonal friction, onboarding, hybrid work, or cross-team collaboration.
Action guidance: Surface and discuss team norms explicitly rather than assuming shared understanding. Involve the whole team in defining and refining them. Revisit norms periodically, especially after growth, conflict, or major change. If norms are only discussed during onboarding and then forgotten, they will not shape behavior.
```
