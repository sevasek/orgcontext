## Definition

Feedback Culture is an organizational environment where giving and receiving honest, specific, timely, and actionable feedback is normal, expected, and safe. It flows in all directions — upward, downward, and peer-to-peer — and is treated as a core driver of learning, performance, and growth rather than an annual HR ritual.

It rests on **psychological safety** as a foundation: people must believe they will not be punished, embarrassed, or retaliated against for speaking candidly. In a strong feedback culture, feedback is not perceived as criticism but as care for performance and personal development. It is frequent, specific, behavior-focused, and solution-oriented.

High feedback cultures (e.g., Netflix, Bridgewater Associates, Google in its best periods) treat feedback as a daily practice rather than an event. They distinguish clearly between **radical candor** (care personally + challenge directly) and the two failure modes: sugarcoating (ruinous empathy) or brutal honesty (obnoxious aggression).

---

## When to Apply

- **Team performance plateaus or stagnation:** When individuals or teams stop improving despite having clear goals.
- **Post-project reviews and retrospectives:** To turn retros into genuine learning opportunities instead of complaint sessions.
- **Leadership development and coaching:** Helping managers move from conflict-avoidant or overly harsh styles to effective feedback.
- **Onboarding and team formation:** Setting explicit norms early so new members know feedback is expected and safe.
- **Conflict resolution or low trust situations:** When unspoken frustrations are damaging relationships or delivery.

---

## Decision Heuristics

- If feedback only flows downward from managers, you do not have a feedback culture — you have a hierarchy.
- If people say "no one ever tells me anything," treat it as a symptom of low feedback culture, not low engagement.
- Feedback should be **specific and recent** — "You did a great job" is praise, not feedback. "In yesterday's meeting, the way you handled the objection on X helped close the discussion productively" is feedback.
- The best indicator of a healthy feedback culture is when people proactively ask for feedback, not just receive it.
- Leaders must model receiving feedback well. If leaders become defensive, the entire culture collapses.

---

## Counter-Examples / Anti-Patterns

- **Niceness culture:** Feedback is avoided or heavily sugarcoated to preserve harmony. Problems fester and mediocrity becomes the norm.
- **Annual review theater:** Feedback is saved for once-a-year formal reviews and delivered as a surprise. This creates anxiety and defensiveness.
- **Brutal honesty / "radical transparency" without care:** Direct feedback delivered without empathy or context, which destroys psychological safety.
- **Feedback as weapon:** Using "I'm just giving you feedback" as cover for personal attacks or political maneuvering.
- **One-way feedback:** Only managers give feedback; employees are discouraged from giving upward or peer feedback.

## Prompt Snippet

```
[ORGANIZATIONAL CONTEXT: Feedback Culture]
Definition: An environment where honest, specific, timely, and actionable feedback flows naturally in all directions (upward, downward, peer-to-peer) and is treated as essential for growth and performance. It requires strong psychological safety as a foundation.
Key principles:
• Feedback must be specific, behavior-focused, recent, and actionable
• Balance care for the person with direct challenge on the work (Radical Candor)
• Feedback is a daily practice, not an annual event
• Both giving and receiving feedback skillfully are expected
When relevant: Performance plateaus, retrospectives, leadership development, onboarding, or when learning/growth has stalled.
Action guidance: Look for signs of avoidance (unsaid frustrations, vague praise only, defensive responses). Encourage frequent, specific feedback. Leaders must model both giving candid feedback and receiving it gracefully. Without psychological safety, feedback initiatives will fail or backfire.
```
